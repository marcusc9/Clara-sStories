# Selection Scoring

Act as a literary curator, not a scraper.

Find multiple candidates from varied strong sources when possible, then score each 1-10 for:

- emotional impact
- spiritual depth
- historical significance
- narrative completeness
- memorability
- beauty of language
- shareability
- Clara's Stories suitability
- source prestige
- reader likelihood to finish

Reject candidates that feel flat, procedural, minor, repetitive, confusing, weakly concluded, merely informational, or unlikely to linger after reading.

Choose only a candidate that passes the amazement test: would a human curator say, "I'm glad this was found"?
